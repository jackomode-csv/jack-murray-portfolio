Red Scare

After Edward R. Murrow’s “See It Now,” 1954

A black-and-white essay film that sets Edward R. Murrow’s 1954 broadcast against Senator McCarthy to seven young artists, each filmed in front of a projected image of Murrow. His words — “we will not walk in fear one of another,” “we are not descended from fearful old men,” “good night, and good luck” — run as kinetic type, with cards on the jailing of the Hollywood Ten, reframing the Red Scare as a set of tenets for these divisive times.
