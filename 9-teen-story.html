9-TEEN

A handcrafted pop-up book that turns flat photography into sculptural, three-dimensional scenes — exploring identity, place, and scale through the Unisphere in Flushing Meadows.

Designer — Jack Murray·2022·Pop-up book, photography·Handcrafted book, typography, paper engineering

The Brief

9-TEEN is a handmade pop-up book that uses photographic imagery and paper engineering to create three-dimensional narratives from flat pages. It merges CT scan imagery, architectural photography of New York’s Unisphere, and hand-cut silhouette figures into a sculptural book form — bound in a silver metallic cover.

Concept

9-TEEN marks the threshold of adulthood — standing at the edge of something vast, looking up. The Unisphere, a relic of the 1964 World’s Fair, recurs as a monument to possibility, while the anonymous hand-cut figures stand in for the viewer.

Process · 01

Prototyped flat, before the fold

Each mechanism was worked out in plain card before being transferred to photographic prints, shot from multiple angles to fold and rise from the page.

Process · 02

The mechanism mid-rise

Seen from the side, the engineering reads clearly — the dimension exists only in the gutter. Flatten the book and the scene disappears.

Process · 03

A cover that echoes the steel

The silver metallic cover was chosen to echo the Unisphere’s stainless steel, grounding the book’s material language in its subject. Closed, it reads as a single brushed-metal object.
