ReflectUS

A children’s-book parable that exposes how American politics keeps recycling the same four stories — and fails to learn from any of them.

Designer — Jack Murray·2025–2026·Children’s book, illustrated parable·Author, narrative direction, illustration with Jaja Riley

The Brief

Reich names four stories American politicians tell on a loop. I wanted to make those stories visible — so I built them into a children’s book, the one format that gets away with repeating itself until you can’t unsee the pattern.

Concept

I set the whole book in one town, Usonia, governed by LIER, and drew it three ways — Castle, Town, Mirror. Same place, three faces. That constraint did the heavy lifting: US and THEM, hope and fear, all play out on one stage.

Process · 01

Mapping one place, read three ways

Before drawing a single page, I mapped every beat to one of three views of the town. If a scene didn’t fit Castle, Town, or Mirror, it didn’t belong in the book.

Process · 02

The Rockwell method

Adapting Norman Rockwell’s blocking technique, I photographed and staged each scene, then handed the layout to illustrator Jaja Riley to bring the characters to life.

Process · 03

A tone borrowed from the fable

I pulled the voice from Orwell’s Animal Farm and mid-century political illustration, rewriting lines until they passed both tests: a kid could read them aloud, and an adult would flinch.
