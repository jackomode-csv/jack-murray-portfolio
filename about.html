About

I make dense ideas into things you can hold.

Jack Murray is a New York–based designer, filmmaker, and bookmaker working in the space between an argument and an image.

I make films, books, and websites that take a dense idea — a Supreme Court ruling, a farm subsidy, the stories a country keeps telling about itself — and turn it into something you can watch, hold, or walk through. The work moves between motion, print, photography, and code, but it always starts the same way: with reading. I research a subject until I understand the argument, then look for the form that makes it felt rather than just explained.

That has meant hand-binding a fifty-page publication on the Supreme Court, flying a drone over a violin built from barn trash, engineering a pop-up book out of flat photographs, and building a political database from real quotes and newspapers. I earned my degree at Parsons School of Design in New York, with a semester at Paris College of Art, and I work across whatever craft a project demands — direction and cinematography, editorial and type design, animation, paper engineering, photography, and front-end development. I believe in earning real expertise — going deep enough in each medium to be trusted with the work and to deliver craft that holds up.

Most of what I make circles the same questions: identity and place, power and persuasion, and the distance between what America says and what it does. I’m drawn to ideas that are usually kept dry — policy, law, design history — and to the problem of making them land for someone who never meant to care.
