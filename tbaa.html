The Project

To / Be / An American is a political project and a database. It presents qualitative data showing that, no matter the politician, these stories exist on both sides, and that we choose to attack each other.

The project informs us about the biases we hold toward leaders who align with us, told through real quotes and newspapers drawn from our shared history.

America’s Four Stories

The concept poster frames every political story through four recurring American narratives.

The Triumphant Individual

The tale of the little guy who works hard, takes risks, believes in himself, and eventually gains wealth, fame, and honor.

The Rot at the Top

A tale of corruption, decadence, and irresponsibility in high places, a conspiracy against the citizen.

The Benevolent Community

The story of neighbors and friends who roll up their sleeves and pitch in for the common good.

The Mob at the Gates

The United States as a beacon light of virtue in a world of darkness, uniquely blessed but continuously endangered by foreign menaces.

Inspiration

Inspired by Forensic Architecture and the Repression Index databases, methods of holding power to account through structured, public evidence.

A study of the importance of the first drafts of history and the complete story, gathered from a wide range of potential sources.

How Maps Change

Pulling back the layers of our own history, maps change like perspectives on history, and this partisan cycle has always been present.

The site originally would have used this map of Washington DC, where each line interacted with would bring up a separate story tied to a multitude of politicians. This proved to be a scale far beyond my capabilities, leading me to focus on presidents.

The Database

The goal was to give viewers the first draft of history from different sides of the story, and to show how perspectives shift over time. This was narrowed down into an early rough sketch of the database.

Wireframes

Each entry pairs positive coverage from the start of a term with negative coverage from the end, and positive quotes from a president about a group against negative quotes about that same group.

From here I recognized my own bias and added midterm coverage of the State of the Unions, since these were the nuts and bolts the president was actually dealing with.

Programming

The project is written in HTML and CSS, with data sourcing done with the aid of Python. Programming was assisted by Claude, across 2,853 lines of code.
