TO / BE / AN AMERICAN

A political database surfacing the biases we hold toward leaders who align with us — told through real quotes and newspapers from our shared history.

Designer — Jack Murray·2025–2026·Website, database·Research, design, development

The Brief

A political project and database presenting qualitative data that, no matter the politician, these stories exist on both sides — and that we choose to attack each other instead of seeing it.

Concept

Every political story is framed through four recurring American narratives — the Triumphant Individual, the Rot at the Top, the Benevolent Community, and the Mob at the Gates.

Process · 01

Built on the first drafts of history

Inspired by Forensic Architecture and the Index of Repression — holding power to account through structured, public evidence gathered from a wide range of sources.

Process · 02

Maps change like perspectives

An early interactive map of Washington DC tied each line to a story. The scale outran the build, so the project narrowed to focus on presidents.

Process · 03

Coverage paired by term

Each entry pairs positive coverage from a term's start with negative coverage from its end — then adds midterm State of the Union framing to check my own bias.

Select Spreads

Process pages from the project book — from inspiration and maps through to wireframes and the built database.
